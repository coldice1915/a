[TL;DR] What's James Han's story?

    His dad has worked with computers and the home he grew up in was filled with electronic devices. When he was 6, together with his older brother they discovered a responsive blank screen on top of an IBM desktop. Every key that was pressed on the keyboard would magically transfer onto the screen. As rewarding as that sounds, they also discovered how to boot that desktop with a floppy disc and learned to play their first game on a pc, Dune 2000. Fast forward a few years, and a game called Starcraft came into the picture. Virtually obsessed with the game, James and his brother would wait for the lights to turn off to play a few local games. There were days when Starcraft didn’t perform as it should and days when they were unable to play on LAN together. It was instances like these that got him thinking, "it's supposed to be working, it always has. What is going on?"

    Expecting something to work and looking forward to its result is completely normal. In fact, it's too normal that when the result does not meet the expectation it springs confusion and hidden stress. This was also known as "problem". James had two choices whenever solving problems: to figure it out with his current knowledge and know-hows, or to seek knowledge and direction from true sources. The latter almost always ends up in the problem being solved and gaining significant knowledge.

    Relationships between people are more significant than the other things of this world. Why that is, was the question at hand and James researched further into this phenomena at Binghamton University, focusing on neuroscience to try to understand the psychology and the biology of why people are intrinsically drawn to each other and the value of each person thereof. Furthermore, the practicality of how to optimally fulfill that quest.

    It wasn’t until much later that James came to realize that people are drawn to computers in a similar way people are drawn to other people. One reason could be the necessity of a computer to connect two people that are physically apart from each other. Another, could be because computers are becoming more anthropomorphous, taking the place of humans in terms of value and relational efficiency. With advances in general technology and artificial intelligence, this discovered phenomena is of no surprise. James recently has begun to continue his education surrounding computers and programming to stay on top and ahead of this new virtual reality and its ubiquitous impact on every life today.

    James Han: I love to be around others and making everyone feel comfortable. One simple problem that I ran into, and later realized affected every relationship I've been in, was misunderstanding and miscommunication. I had resolved to find a cure and have narrowed my search into the subjects of the mind and simple math.
