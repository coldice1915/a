[TL;DR] What's your story?

    My dad has always worked in IT so naturally I had a lot of electronic devices around the house growing up, including computers. When I was in elementary school, my brother and I would stay up until the lights turned off to play a local game of Starcraft against computers, or NPCs. There were days when Starcraft didn’t perform as it should or days my brother and I couldn’t play together on LAN. It was instances like these that nurtured my critical thinking skills to solve real life problems; it was pretty serious then. I understood that there was always an answer to a problem, it was only a matter of skillful deduction and level of relative difficulty; from the easy (the Starcraft CD needed to be cleaned) to the hard (having to install TCP/IP protocol for the computer to communicate to an interconnected device so that my brother and I could play together on LAN). My love for computers would go on into adulthood and by then I’ve encountered countless errors and problems that were not left unsolved. But the growth of a computer-related career was not in my agenda at the time as it was something I enjoyed, rather I felt the need to pursue a career in helping others in a “face-to-face” manner.

    Relationships between people are significant compared to any other relationship between anything else in the world. During my time at Binghamton University, I researched further into this phenomena to try to understand the psychology and neuroscience of why people are intrinsically drawn to each other and how to optimally fulfill that interaction and the existence of both parties. This desire had led me to choose a career path in therapy to first-handedly serve and interact with others.

    It wasn’t until mid-2019, when I was baffled at the understanding of another reality with its own set of laws and rules and how ubiquitous it is on every sector imaginable. This reality, called programming, presents us with the space and ability to materialize our hopes and dreams in the form of an application or client that humans will inevitably interact with. I want to utilize my knowledge in human behavior to service people and better our relationships those around us. Impacting each individual to categorically transform generations to come.

[TL;DR] What do you hope to accomplish?

    Serve the One who loved me first by serving others, expecting nothing back.
